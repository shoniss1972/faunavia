# Faunavia — Game Concept

## One-sentence hook

Rescue, load, and transport a cast of opinionated wild animals across challenging terrain by choosing the right vehicle, equipment, and travelling companions.

## Overview

Faunavia is a mobile-first wildlife rescue game combining short driving challenges, route planning, vehicle upgrades, and animal-compatibility puzzles.

The player begins with a bicycle and small rear cage, taking simple jobs involving small, cooperative animals. Successful missions unlock motorcycles, flat-deck vehicles, jeeps, trucks, trailers, and specialised transport equipment capable of handling larger animals, difficult terrain, and more complicated passenger combinations.

Before each mission, the player studies the route and animals, then selects the vehicle, cage configuration, supplies, and handling equipment. During the journey, the player collects animals, manages fuel and food, stops at vet or supply stations when needed, and keeps passengers comfortable enough to reach a sanctuary or release point.

## Target player

Casual mobile players who enjoy:

- short, replayable levels;
- vehicle and equipment upgrades;
- sorting, matching, and unblock puzzles;
- simple terrain and driving challenges;
- collecting animals and unlocking content;
- expressive characters and gentle humour.

A typical level should last two to five minutes.

## Core loop

**Prepare → travel → collect → load → manage → deliver → upgrade**

The player balances:

- vehicle capacity and weight;
- animal compatibility;
- fuel consumption;
- route difficulty;
- suitable cages and trailers;
- handling equipment;
- food, water, and supplies;
- animal comfort.

The largest vehicle is not always the best solution. Larger vehicles carry more but consume more fuel, handle poorly on narrow or steep tracks, and may require stronger brakes, engines, tyres, or suspension.

## Distinctive features

### Opinionated animal passengers

Animals remain recognisable wildlife but are slightly anthropomorphic and highly expressive. They glare at unsuitable cage mates, refuse inadequate transport, celebrate preferred food, complain about poor suspension, and silently judge bad preparation.

Predators and prey never attack one another. Incompatible animals object, become uncooperative, or refuse to travel until separated.

### Vehicle progression changes the puzzle

Vehicle progression may include:

- bicycle with rear cage;
- motorcycle;
- small flat deck;
- jeep with enclosed cage;
- wildlife truck;
- heavy truck with specialist trailers.

Upgrade categories include fuel capacity, engine, brakes, suspension, tyres, cages, cage dividers, ramps, feed storage, tow hitches, trailers, and aquatic tanks.

Upgrades create trade-offs. A trailer adds capacity and separation but worsens braking, climbing, turning, stability, and fuel use. An aquatic tank transports penguins and other water-dependent animals but is heavy and destabilising.

### Rescue logistics, not just driving

Missions may require the player to:

- detour for fuel;
- replenish feed, bait, or water at a food store;
- visit a vet station for treatment or a precautionary check;
- choose between a steep shortcut and a longer safe route;
- separate incompatible animals;
- carry nets, leashes, gloves, ramps, or specialist containers;
- decide between one difficult trip and several smaller trips.

## Tone and safety

The world is warm, playful, optimistic, and family-friendly.

**No visible injury, suffering, capture trauma, predation, or death.** Negative consequences are expressed through comedy, inconvenience, animal refusal, lost efficiency, reduced comfort, or a safe restart.

Animal states include annoyed, nervous, suspicious, impatient, smug, comfortable, and delighted—not injured or in pain.

## Initial prototype

The prototype should include only:

- three vehicles: bicycle, jeep, and truck;
- six animals with distinct size, weight, and compatibility traits;
- one trailer;
- fuel, weight, capacity, comfort, and compatibility mechanics;
- cages, dividers, ramps, feed, nets, leashes, and gloves as simple data choices;
- a fuel stop, food store, vet station, and sanctuary;
- 10–15 short levels;
- simple driving and humorous reactions;
- no advertising, purchases, multiplayer, accounts, or live-service systems.

## Success test

The concept earns further development only when unfamiliar players:

- understand the objective without explanation;
- enjoy choosing equipment and arranging animals;
- replay failed or low-scoring levels voluntarily;
- learn and anticipate animal behaviours;
- want to unlock the next vehicle;
- ask for more levels.
